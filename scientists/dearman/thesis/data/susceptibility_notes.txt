Notes on Susceptibility Values (indicative; temperature and purity dependent)

- water: χ ≈ −9×10^−6 (diamagnetic)
- bismuth: χ ≈ −1.66×10^−4 (strong diamagnet)
- pyrolytic graphite: χ ≈ −4×10^−4 (anisotropic; value is along strong axis)
- oxygen (gas): χ ≈ +1.9×10^−6 (paramagnetic at ~300 K)
- mercury (liquid): χ ≈ −3.3×10^−5 (diamagnetic)
- gold: χ ≈ −3.6×10^−5 (diamagnetic)
- silver: χ ≈ −2.4×10^−5 (diamagnetic)
- diamond: χ ≈ −2.1×10^−5 (diamagnetic)
- helium (gas): χ ≈ −2×10^−9 (very weak diamagnet)
- silica (SiO2): χ ≈ −1.1×10^−5 (diamagnetic)
- iron: χ varies strongly with field; low-field differential susceptibility can be ≫1 before saturation
- magnetite (Fe3O4): χ ≈ 10^−2 (order of magnitude; depends on microstructure)
